Yemenite Zalabyot - Fried Dough Fritters

Ingredients
- 2 cups / 250g all-purpose flour
- 1 tsp active dry yeast
- 1 tsp sugar
- 1 cup warm water
- 1/2 tsp salt
- Vegetable oil for deep frying

For serving:
- Honey or date syrup (silan)
- Powdered sugar
- Optional: schug or grated tomato for a savory version

Instructions
In a bowl, combine the warm water, yeast and sugar. Stir and let sit for 5 minutes until foamy.

Add the flour and salt to the yeast mixture and whisk vigorously until a smooth, thin batter forms (similar to pancake batter). Cover and let rest in a warm place for 30-45 minutes until bubbles form on the surface.

Heat vegetable oil in a deep pot or pan to about 350°F / 175°C. The oil should be about 5cm / 2 inches deep.

Using a large spoon or ladle, carefully drop spoonfuls of batter into the hot oil. Fry for 2-3 minutes until golden on the bottom, then flip and fry another 2 minutes until golden all over. Do not overcrowd the pan.

Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.

Serve immediately drizzled with honey or silan and dusted with powdered sugar.

Description
Zalabyot are traditional Yemenite fried dough fritters, similar to donuts or funnel cake. They are traditionally served at Hanukkah and Mimouna celebrations, as well as on Shabbat morning alongside kubaniyot. Crispy on the outside, airy on the inside, and irresistible when drizzled with honey or silan.
