Israeli Biscuit Cake

Ingredients
- 2 packs of tea biscuits (400g total)
- 200g / 7oz dark or milk chocolate, chopped
- 1 cup heavy cream
- 3 tbsp butter
- 3 tbsp sugar
- 2 tbsp cocoa powder
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- Optional: 1/2 cup chopped walnuts or almonds
- Optional: sprinkles or crushed biscuits for topping

Instructions
In a saucepan over low heat, combine the heavy cream, butter, sugar and cocoa powder. Stir until the butter melts and the mixture is smooth. Remove from heat and add the chopped chocolate and vanilla. Stir until the chocolate is fully melted and the mixture is glossy and smooth. Let cool for 10 minutes.

Line a loaf pan or rectangular container with plastic wrap, leaving extra wrap hanging over the sides.

Dip each biscuit briefly in milk or coffee (optional) and arrange a layer in the bottom of the pan. Pour a layer of chocolate cream over the biscuits. Repeat with another layer of biscuits and another layer of chocolate cream. Continue until you run out of ingredients, finishing with a chocolate layer on top.

Cover with the overhanging plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight until firm.

Unmold onto a serving plate, remove plastic wrap, and decorate with sprinkles or crushed biscuits if desired.

Description
Israeli biscuit cake (ugat bishkitim) is one of the most nostalgic and beloved no-bake desserts in Israel. Almost every Israeli grew up eating this at birthday parties and family gatherings. It is incredibly easy to make, requires no baking, and is deeply satisfying. A true taste of Israeli childhood.
