Cheese Blintzes with Berry Sauce

Ingredients
For the crepes:
- 1 cup / 125g all-purpose flour
- 1.5 cups milk
- 2 eggs
- 1 tbsp sugar
- 1 tbsp butter, melted
- 1/4 tsp salt
- Butter or oil for frying

For the cheese filling:
- 500g / 17oz farmer's cheese or ricotta
- 3 tbsp sugar
- 1 egg yolk
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- Zest of half a lemon

For the berry sauce:
- 2 cups fresh or frozen mixed berries (strawberries, blueberries, raspberries)
- 3 tbsp sugar
- 1 tbsp lemon juice
- 1 tsp cornstarch dissolved in 2 tbsp water

Instructions
Make the crepe batter: whisk together flour, milk, eggs, sugar, melted butter and salt until smooth. Rest for 30 minutes in the fridge.

Make the filling: mix together the farmer's cheese, sugar, egg yolk, vanilla and lemon zest until smooth and combined.

Make the berry sauce: combine berries, sugar and lemon juice in a saucepan over medium heat. Cook for 5 minutes until the berries break down. Add the cornstarch mixture and stir until the sauce thickens. Remove from heat.

Cook the crepes: heat a non-stick pan over medium heat and lightly grease. Pour a thin layer of batter (about 3 tbsp) and swirl to coat the pan. Cook for 1-2 minutes until the edges lift, flip and cook 30 more seconds. Stack on a plate.

Fill and fold: place 2 tbsp of cheese filling in the center of each crepe. Fold the sides in and roll into a neat rectangular package.

In a pan with butter, fry the blintzes seam-side down for 2-3 minutes per side until golden and crispy.

Serve hot topped with warm berry sauce and a dusting of powdered sugar.

Description
Cheese blintzes are a cherished Ashkenazi Jewish dish traditionally served on Shavuot, celebrating the holiday's dairy tradition. Crispy on the outside with a creamy, lightly sweetened cheese filling inside, and topped with vibrant berry sauce — they are as beautiful as they are delicious. A true labor of love worth every step.
