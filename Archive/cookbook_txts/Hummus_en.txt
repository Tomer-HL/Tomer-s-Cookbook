Hummus

Ingredients
- 2 cups dried chickpeas (soaked overnight) or 2 cans of cooked chickpeas (400g each)
- 1/2 cup raw tahini
- 3-4 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 2-3 cloves garlic
- 1 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp cumin
- 4-6 tbsp ice cold water (as needed)
- 2 tbsp olive oil (for serving)
- Paprika and fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions
If using dried chickpeas: drain the soaked chickpeas and cook in fresh water for 60-90 minutes until very soft. Reserve 1 cup of the cooking liquid. If using canned chickpeas: drain and rinse, reserving a few tablespoons of liquid.

Set aside a small handful of whole chickpeas for garnish.

In a food processor, blend the garlic until finely minced. Add the tahini and lemon juice and blend for 1 minute until the mixture turns pale and creamy.

Add the chickpeas, salt and cumin. Blend for 3-4 minutes, scraping the sides occasionally. While blending, add ice cold water tablespoon by tablespoon until you reach a smooth, creamy texture. The longer you blend, the smoother the hummus.

Taste and adjust seasoning — more lemon, salt or garlic as needed.

Spread into a shallow bowl, drizzle generously with olive oil, sprinkle paprika and garnish with whole chickpeas and chopped parsley.

Description
Hummus is arguably the most iconic dish in Israeli and Middle Eastern cuisine. The secret to great hummus is using quality tahini, blending for a long time, and adding ice cold water. Serve fresh and at room temperature for the best flavor and texture.
