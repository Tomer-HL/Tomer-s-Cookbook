Rolled Cookies - Dates and Chocolate

Ingredients
For the dough:
- 3 cups / 375g all-purpose flour
- 1/2 cup vegetable oil
- 1/2 cup warm water
- 2 tbsp sugar
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 1 tsp vanilla extract

For the date filling:
- 300g / 10oz pitted Medjool dates
- 2 tbsp butter or coconut oil
- 1/2 tsp cinnamon
- Pinch of cardamom
- 2-3 tbsp water

For the chocolate filling:
- 100g / 3.5oz dark chocolate, melted
- 2 tbsp cocoa powder
- 2 tbsp sugar
- 2 tbsp butter, melted

For topping:
- 1 egg, beaten
- Sesame seeds or powdered sugar

Instructions
Make the dough: combine all dough ingredients and knead for 5 minutes until smooth. Cover and rest for 30 minutes.

Make the date filling: combine dates, butter, cinnamon, cardamom and water in a small saucepan. Cook over low heat, mashing with a fork, until you have a smooth paste. Let cool.

Make the chocolate filling: mix together the melted chocolate, cocoa, sugar and melted butter until smooth.

Preheat oven to 350°F / 180°C. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

Divide the dough in half. Roll each half into a thin rectangle. Spread your chosen filling evenly over the surface, leaving a 1cm border. Roll tightly into a log and slice into 1.5cm rounds.

Place on the baking sheet, brush with beaten egg and sprinkle with sesame seeds.

Bake for 18-22 minutes until golden. Cool completely before serving.

Description
These traditional rolled cookies are a staple of Israeli holiday baking, especially during Rosh Hashanah and Purim. The date filling version carries North African roots while the chocolate version is a crowd-pleaser for all ages. Both versions are tender, slightly flaky and incredibly satisfying.
