Chocolate Rugelach

Ingredients
For the dough:
- 2 cups / 250g all-purpose flour
- 200g / 7oz cold butter, cubed
- 200g / 7oz cream cheese, cold
- 2 tbsp sugar
- 1/4 tsp salt

For the chocolate filling:
- 100g / 3.5oz dark chocolate, finely chopped or grated
- 1/4 cup sugar
- 2 tsp cinnamon
- 3 tbsp cocoa powder
- 2 tbsp butter, melted

For brushing:
- 1 egg, beaten
- 2 tbsp sugar mixed with 1 tsp cinnamon

Instructions
Make the dough: in a food processor, pulse together the flour, salt and sugar. Add the cold butter and cream cheese and pulse until the dough just comes together. Do not over-process. Divide into two discs, wrap in plastic and refrigerate for at least 1 hour or overnight.

Mix together the chocolate filling ingredients in a bowl.

Preheat oven to 350°F / 180°C. Line two baking sheets with parchment paper.

On a lightly floured surface, roll out one dough disc into a circle about 30cm / 12 inches in diameter. Brush with melted butter and sprinkle half the filling evenly over the surface, leaving a small border.

Cut the circle into 12-16 wedges like a pizza. Roll each wedge from the wide end to the tip. Place on the baking sheet with the tip tucked underneath.

Brush with beaten egg and sprinkle with cinnamon sugar.

Bake for 20-25 minutes until golden. Let cool before serving.

Description
Rugelach are one of the most iconic Jewish pastries, beloved across Israel and around the world. These crescent-shaped cookies have a flaky, buttery cream cheese dough wrapped around a rich chocolate and cinnamon filling. They are a staple of Jewish holidays and celebrations and disappear within minutes of being served.
