Moroccan Fish

Ingredients
- 4-6 fish fillets (sea bass, tilapia, or any firm white fish)
- 4 tbsp olive oil
- 6-8 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
- 1 red bell pepper, sliced
- 1 green bell pepper, sliced
- 1 cup crushed tomatoes or 3-4 fresh ripe tomatoes, diced
- 1/2 cup water
- 1 tsp paprika
- 1 tsp sweet paprika
- 1/2 tsp cumin
- 1/2 tsp turmeric
- 1/4 tsp cayenne or chili flakes (adjust to taste)
- 1 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- Fresh cilantro or parsley, chopped, for garnish
- 1 lemon, sliced

Instructions
Heat olive oil in a wide, deep pan over medium heat. Add the garlic slices and sauté for 1-2 minutes until fragrant, being careful not to burn them.

Add the sliced bell peppers and sauté for 3-4 minutes until slightly softened.

Add the crushed tomatoes, water, paprika, sweet paprika, cumin, turmeric, cayenne, salt and black pepper. Stir well and bring to a simmer. Cook the sauce for 10 minutes until it thickens slightly.

Taste the sauce and adjust seasoning. Gently place the fish fillets into the sauce, spooning some sauce over the top of each fillet.

Cover the pan and cook on medium-low heat for 12-15 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through and flakes easily.

Garnish with fresh cilantro or parsley and lemon slices. Serve hot with fresh bread or couscous.

Description
Moroccan fish is a staple of Israeli home cooking, brought by Jewish immigrants from North Africa. The fragrant tomato and pepper sauce, spiced with cumin, turmeric and paprika, is what makes this dish unforgettable. It is traditionally served on Shabbat and holidays, and pairs beautifully with fresh challah to soak up the sauce.
