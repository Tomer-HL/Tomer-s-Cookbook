Moroccan Matbucha

Ingredients
- 6 ripe tomatoes, diced
- 2 red bell peppers, roasted, peeled and diced
- 6-8 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
- 4 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp paprika
- 1 tsp sweet paprika
- 1/2 tsp cumin
- 1/2 tsp chili flakes or 1 fresh chili pepper, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tsp sugar
- Fresh parsley or cilantro for garnish

Instructions
To roast the peppers: place them directly over a gas flame or under a broiler, turning occasionally, until the skin is completely charred. Place in a sealed bag for 10 minutes, then peel, seed and dice them.

Heat olive oil in a wide heavy pan over medium heat. Add the garlic and sauté for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.

Add the diced tomatoes, roasted peppers, paprika, sweet paprika, cumin, chili, salt and sugar. Stir well.

Cook uncovered on medium-low heat, stirring occasionally, for 45-60 minutes until the mixture thickens and most of the liquid has evaporated. The matbucha should be jammy and concentrated.

Taste and adjust seasoning. Let cool and serve at room temperature or cold, garnished with fresh parsley.

Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days.

Description
Matbucha is a slow-cooked Moroccan tomato and pepper relish that is a cornerstone of Israeli mezze culture. It is served as a dip with fresh bread, alongside hummus and other salads. The long cooking time is what gives matbucha its deep, rich flavor — patience is the secret ingredient.
