Yemenite Kubaniyot - Overnight Rolls

Ingredients
- 4 cups / 500g all-purpose flour
- 2.5 tsp active dry yeast (or 1 packet)
- 1 cup warm water
- 3 tbsp sugar
- 1.5 tsp salt
- 1/3 cup vegetable oil or melted butter
- 1 egg
- Softened butter for spreading between layers

Instructions
In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast and sugar in warm water. Let sit for 5-10 minutes until foamy.

In a large bowl, combine flour and salt. Add the yeast mixture, oil and egg. Mix and knead for about 8 minutes until a smooth, soft dough forms. Cover and let rise for 1 hour until doubled.

Divide the dough into 8-10 equal balls. Flatten each ball into a thin circle and spread a thin layer of softened butter on top. Roll up each circle into a log, then coil into a round roll shape.

Place the rolls in a greased heavy pot (traditionally a cast iron pot) with a lid. The rolls should fit snugly together.

For overnight baking (traditional method): cover tightly and place in an oven set to the lowest temperature (around 200°F / 93°C) before Shabbat. Bake overnight for 10-12 hours. The rolls will be dark, caramelized and incredibly soft.

For regular baking: cover and let rise 30 minutes, then bake at 350°F / 180°C for 25-30 minutes until golden.

Serve warm with schug, honey, grated tomato or hard boiled eggs.

Description
Kubaniyot are the iconic Yemenite overnight rolls traditionally baked slowly throughout the Shabbat night. The long slow bake caramelizes the dough and creates a uniquely sweet, soft and slightly chewy texture unlike any other bread. They are a beloved staple of the Yemenite Shabbat morning table.
