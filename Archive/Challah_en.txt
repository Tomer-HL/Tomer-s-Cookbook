Challah

Ingredients
- 4 cups / 500g all-purpose flour, plus more for kneading
- 2.5 tsp active dry yeast (or 1 packet)
- 1 cup warm water (not hot)
- 1/4 cup sugar
- 1/3 cup vegetable oil
- 2 eggs (1 for dough, 1 for brushing)
- 1.5 tsp salt
- Sesame seeds or poppy seeds for topping (optional)

Instructions
In a small bowl, mix the warm water, sugar and yeast. Let sit for 5-10 minutes until foamy and active.

In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and add the yeast mixture, oil and 1 egg. Mix until a dough forms, then turn out onto a floured surface and knead for 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic. The dough should be soft but not sticky.

Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a damp towel or plastic wrap, and let rise in a warm place for 1-1.5 hours until doubled in size.

Punch down the dough and divide into 3 equal strands. Roll each strand into a long rope. Braid the three strands together, tucking the ends under. Place on a parchment-lined baking sheet.

Cover and let rise for another 45 minutes. Preheat oven to 350°F / 180°C.

Beat the second egg and brush generously over the challah. Sprinkle with sesame or poppy seeds if desired.

Bake for 30-35 minutes until deep golden brown. Let cool on a wire rack before slicing.

Description
Challah is the traditional braided bread of the Jewish Shabbat and holiday table. Its slightly sweet, soft and pillowy texture makes it irresistible for dipping in soup, spreading with butter, or simply eating as is. Making challah from scratch is one of the most rewarding things you can do in the kitchen.
