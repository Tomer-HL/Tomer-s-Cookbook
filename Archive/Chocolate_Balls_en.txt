Israeli Chocolate Balls

Ingredients
- 200g / 7oz tea biscuits, finely crushed
- 3 tbsp cocoa powder
- 1/2 cup powdered sugar
- 3 tbsp butter, softened
- 3 tbsp milk or heavy cream
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- Optional: 1 tbsp rum or brandy

For rolling:
- Shredded coconut
- Cocoa powder
- Sprinkles
- Crushed nuts

Instructions
Crush the biscuits into fine crumbs using a food processor or by placing them in a zip-lock bag and rolling with a rolling pin.

In a large bowl, mix together the biscuit crumbs, cocoa powder and powdered sugar. Add the softened butter, milk and vanilla (and alcohol if using). Mix well until the mixture comes together into a firm dough. If it is too dry, add a little more milk. If too wet, add more crushed biscuits.

Refrigerate the mixture for 30 minutes until firm enough to roll.

With slightly wet hands, roll the mixture into small balls about 2.5cm / 1 inch in diameter.

Roll each ball in your choice of coating — coconut, cocoa, sprinkles or crushed nuts. Place on a tray lined with parchment paper.

Refrigerate for at least 1 hour before serving. Keep refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 5 days.

Description
Israeli chocolate balls (kadoorey shokolad) are a beloved no-bake treat that every Israeli child knows. They are a staple at birthday parties, holiday celebrations and school events. Simple, fun to make, and utterly delicious — especially when rolled in coconut.
