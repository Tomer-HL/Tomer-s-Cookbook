Israeli Baked Cheesecake

Ingredients
For the crust:
- 200g / 7oz digestive biscuits or graham crackers, crushed
- 80g / 3oz butter, melted
- 2 tbsp sugar

For the filling:
- 800g / 28oz cream cheese (full fat), at room temperature
- 3/4 cup sugar
- 3 eggs
- 1/2 cup sour cream
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 2 tbsp cornstarch
- Zest of 1 lemon
- 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice

Instructions
Preheat oven to 325°F / 160°C. Grease a 23cm / 9-inch springform pan.

Make the crust: mix crushed biscuits, melted butter and sugar together. Press firmly and evenly into the bottom of the pan. Bake for 10 minutes, then let cool.

Make the filling: beat the cream cheese and sugar together until smooth and creamy. Add the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each. Add the sour cream, vanilla, cornstarch, lemon zest and lemon juice. Mix until just combined — do not over-mix.

Pour the filling over the cooled crust. Tap the pan gently on the counter to release air bubbles.

Bake for 55-65 minutes until the edges are set but the center still has a slight wobble. Turn off the oven, crack the door open, and let the cheesecake cool inside for 1 hour. This prevents cracking.

Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight before serving.

Description
This classic Israeli baked cheesecake is a staple of Shavuot celebrations but beloved year-round. Rich, creamy and perfectly tangy from the lemon, it is everything a cheesecake should be. The key is not to over-mix, bake gently, and let it cool slowly in the oven to prevent cracks.
