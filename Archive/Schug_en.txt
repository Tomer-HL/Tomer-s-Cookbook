Schug - Yemenite Hot Sauce

Ingredients
- 6-8 fresh green or red chili peppers (adjust to desired heat)
- 1 full head of garlic, peeled
- 1 large bunch of fresh cilantro (stems included)
- 1/2 bunch of fresh parsley
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp coriander
- 1/2 tsp cardamom
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp salt
- 3-4 tbsp olive oil
- Juice of half a lemon

Instructions
Wash the chili peppers and cilantro thoroughly. Remove the stems from the chilies but keep the seeds for maximum heat (remove seeds for a milder version).

Place the chilies and garlic in a food processor and pulse several times until roughly chopped.

Add the cilantro, parsley, cumin, coriander, cardamom, black pepper, salt and lemon juice. Process until you reach a coarse paste — do not over-blend, some texture is preferred.

Add the olive oil and pulse a few more times to combine.

Taste and adjust seasoning. Transfer to a glass jar and drizzle a thin layer of olive oil on top to preserve freshness.

Store in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks.

Description
Schug is the iconic Yemenite hot sauce that has become a staple condiment across all of Israel. It comes in green (cilantro-based) and red (dried chili-based) versions. Schug adds fire and depth to everything — shakshuka, falafel, sabich, grilled meats, soup and more. A little goes a long way!
