Yemenite Beef Soup

Ingredients
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2-3 medium onions, peeled and diced
- 2 lbs beef stew meat with bones
- 1 tbsp paprika
- 1/2 tsp cumin
- 2 tbsp hawaij spice blend
- 8-10 cloves of garlic
- 2-3 carrots, peeled and cut into 2-inch pieces
- 3-4 medium red or Yukon gold potatoes, peeled and cut into quarters
- 2 celery stalks, cut into 2-inch pieces
- 1 bunch cilantro, cleaned, rinsed, and chopped
- 3-4 whole allspice pieces
- 2 bay leaves
- Salt to taste

Instructions
Place beef pieces in an 8-quart stock pot. Add about 12-14 cups of water (about 2/3 of the way high). Bring to a boil and simmer for a few minutes, skimming the foam that rises to the top.

Add and stir the hawaij, cumin, paprika, salt, onion and garlic cloves into the pot. Reduce heat to medium and let the soup cook for 1 hour 30 minutes until the meat softens. Keep an eye periodically to make sure the simmer is low and bubbling but not boiling too rapidly. Add water if needed.

Add the potato chunks, slices of carrots, celery, allspice, bay leaves and the cilantro. Bring back to a low simmer and continue to cook for 30 minutes more, or until the carrots and potatoes soften.

Recommended: dip challah or traditional Yemenite pita in the soup.

Description
Yemenite beef soup is traditionally served as the entree of the meal on a holiday evening and in social events. The broth is spiced with hawaij, a Yemenite spice blend that can be purchased at most Jewish markets, and is very healthy due to the high amount of turmeric in it.
