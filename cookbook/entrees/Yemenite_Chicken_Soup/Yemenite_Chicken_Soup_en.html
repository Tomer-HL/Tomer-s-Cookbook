Yemenite Chicken Soup

A classic dish you can easily make at home.
Yemenite chicken soup is traditiona ly served as the entree of the Shabbat meal on Friday evening and in social events. The broth of this soup is spiced with hawaij, a Yemenite spice blend that can be purchased at most Jewish markets and very healthy due to the high amount of tumeric in it. Recommended: serve with chalah or traditional Yemenite-pita or Lachuch in the soup.
🕒 Time: 60 minutes
🍽️ Servings: 4
🧑‍🍳 Level: Easy

Ingredients

  • 6 chicken drumsticks or 3-4 chicken legs
  • 1 tbsp hawaij spice
  • 2 onions, peeled and cut into halves or quarters
  • 5-6 cloves of garlic
  • 2–3 carrots, peeled and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and cut into quarters
  • 1–3 celery stalks, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 bunch cilantro, cleaned, rinsed, and finely chopped
  • 1/2 tbsp salt (add more later if needed)

Instructions

  1. Place chicken pieces in an 8-quart stock pot. Add about 12–14 cups of water (about 2/3 full). Bring to a boil and simmer for a few minutes, skimming off the foam that rises to the top.
  2. Add and stir in: hawaij, salt, onions and garlic.
  3. Bring to a boil, then reduce to medium heat and cook for about 15 minutes. Keep it at a gentle simmer (bubbling lightly, not rapidly boiling).
  4. Add the potatoes, carrots, celery, cilantro. Bring back to a low simmer and cook for another 30 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
