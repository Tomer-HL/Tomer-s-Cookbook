Juicy Kebab

Ingredients
600 g fresh ground beef (or a mix of beef and lamb) – preferably from short ribs (asado cut)
1 large onion, grated or very finely chopped
1 bunch of parsley or cilantro, finely chopped
1½ tsp salt
½ tsp baking soda
1 tsp ground black pepper
½ tsp cumin
½ tsp cinnamon
Optional: ¼ tsp baharat
Optional: a handful of finely chopped pine nuts or pistachios.

Instructions
1. Prepare the mixture:
 Place all the ingredients in a large bowl.
 Mix היטב with your hands for 2–3 minutes until the mixture is uniform and slightly sticky (this helps create a juicy, well-bound kebab).
2. Chill:
 Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes (up to 1 hour).
 This helps the flavors develop and the mixture firm up.
3. Shape the kebabs:
 Lightly wet your hands with water or oil.
 Take a portion of the mixture about the size of a small fist.
For skewers:
 Press the meat onto a flat metal skewer and shape it into a long, even kebab.
Without skewers:
 Shape into oval or elongated patties (like an elongated meatball), keeping them uniform in size.
Tip: Do not pack the meat too tightly — it can make the kebabs less juicy.
4. Cook:
 Preheat a grill or pan.
Cook over medium-high heat
About 4–5 minutes per side
Turn only when the kebab releases easily and has a nice sear
Done when: firm to the touch, nicely browned on the outside, and juicy inside

Description
Turkish-style kebab is a beloved street food across the Middle East and Israel. The key to a great kebab is using fresh, high-quality meat and not overworking the mixture. Adding ground lamb gives a richer, more traditional flavor. Best served hot straight off the grill.
